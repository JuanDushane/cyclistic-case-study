# Cyclistic Case Study – Rider Behavior Analysis

## 📊 Project Overview

This case study analyzes how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently.  
The goal is to identify data-driven strategies to convert casual riders into annual members.

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1. Ride Length Differences
Casual riders take longer trips (~36 minutes) compared to members (~15 minutes).  
This suggests casual riders use bikes for leisure, while members use them for commuting.

### 2. Day of Week Behavior
Casual riders prefer weekends, while members ride consistently throughout the week.

### 3. Time of Day Patterns
Members show peak usage during commuting hours.  
Casual riders are more active during midday and afternoons.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
1. Offer weekend promotions to target casual riders  
2. Introduce leisure-focused membership perks  
3. Use time-based promotions (midday/weekends)
